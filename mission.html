We all know that water is of utmost important in this over populating world.
According to the latest data available from the Ministry of Jal Shakti,
Government of India, the total water consumption in India in the year 2017
was 634 billion cubic meters (BCM).

Out of this, the agricultural sector consumed the most water, accounting
for 83% of the total consumption, while the industrial and domestic sectors
accounted for 5% and 12% respectively. It is important to note that water
consumption patterns in India can vary widely depending on the region and the availability of water resources. Some regions of the country face acute water scarcity, while others have an abundance of water resources.

LEVEL-1

For people whose water
bill is BELOW 4000 INR

  • Fix leaks: Even small leaks can waste a lot of water over time. Check your faucets, pipes, and toilets for leaks and repair them as soon as possible..
  • Tell your children not to play with the hose and sprinklers.
  • Don't run the hose while washing your car.
  • Put a layer of mulch around trees and plants.
  • Fix your lawn: Consider xeriscaping or using drought-resistant plants to reduce your lawn's water needs..
  • Deep-soak your lawn.
  • If you wash dishes by hand, don't leave the water running for rinsing
  • Use your automatic washing machine only for full loads only.

LEVEL-2

For people whose water
bill is BELOW 8000 INR

  • Turn off the water while shaving or while brushing your teeth
  • Take shorter showers or use bucket and mug while bathing.
  • Install low-flow fixtures: Replace old faucets, showerheads, and toilets
    with low-flow fixtures. They can help reduce water consumption without
    sacrificing performance.
  • Collect rainwater: Install a rain barrel to collect rainwater that you can
    use for watering plants or washing your car.
  • Use a watering can instead of a hose to water your plants. Water them
    in the morning or evening when it's cooler and less water will evaporate.
  • Use a rain barrel to collect rainwater and use it to water your plants or lawn.
  • Taking shorter showers can help you save water and reduce your bill. Try to limit your shower time to 5 minutes or less.

bill is ABOVE 8000 INR

SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS

OUR COMMITMENT

Water is inextricably linked to the development of all nations.
But unsustainable development is placing pressure on water
resources: by 2030, global demand for water is expected to
increase by 50%. Meanwhile,agriculture (which consumes around
70% of global water usage) is expected to experience a 70%
increase in demand by 2050.

Our aim at DISTIL is to ensure availability and sustainable
management of water and sanitation for all. Access to safe water,
sanitation and hygiene is the most basic human need for health
and well-being. Knowing the fact that in many countries there is a
lack of water, an attempt is made by us here to make the complex
topic of sustainability understandable and practical by creating
awareness.
